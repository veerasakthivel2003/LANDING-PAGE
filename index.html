Cakes:Love at First Bite

Ancient Egypt:
The earliest recorded evidence of cake-making is from ancient Egypt, where cakes were baked as offerings to the gods.

Mid-17th century Europe:
The precursors of modern cakes were first baked in Europe, thanks to advances in technology and access to ingredients. These cakes were round and topped with icing, which was usually a boiled mixture of sugar, egg whites, and flavorings.

19th century:
Cakes became more popular, but were still considered a luxury because sweet ingredients like sugar and chocolate were expensive.

Today:
Cakes have taken on many forms and are enjoyed by people all over the world.

It is a derivation of 'kaka', an Old Norse word. Medieval European bakers often made fruitcakes and gingerbread. These foods could last for many months.
